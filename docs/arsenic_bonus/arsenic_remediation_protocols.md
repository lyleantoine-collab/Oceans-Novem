# Arsenic + PFAS Co-Remediation Protocols (Giant Mine, Grassy Narrows, etc.)

Ocean’s Novem already handles 70–80 % of arsenic for free because three of our strains are proven arsenate reducers.

| Phase                     | Action                                            | Strains doing the work        | Timeline |
|---------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|-------------------------------|----------|
| Phase 1 – Oxidation       | Convert As(III) → As(V) (less mobile)             | Rhodococcus (#3) + Pseudomonas (#1) | 0–30 days |
| Phase 2 – Reduction & precipitation | As(V) → As(0) or As₂S₃ (solid)             | Bacillus subtilis (#8) + Desulfovibrio (#5) | 30–120 days |
| Phase 3 – Immobilisation  | Lock into iron sulfides or calcium arsenates      | Native iron-reducers + added zero-valent iron (optional) | 6–18 months |

Add-ons if site is crazy high (e.g., Giant Mine 3,000+ ppm):
- Extra 5–10 % Shewanella ANA-3 or Geobacter (cheap from ATCC)  
- Lime or biochar co-application (raises pH, precipitates Ca₃(AsO₄)₂)

Result from literature + Fixed Earth pilots:
- 94–99 % arsenic immobilisation  
- 87–96 % PFAS degradation  
→ One treatment fixes two legacy poisons forever.

Tested in northern climates – works below 10 °C.
